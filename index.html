Population data

PS4 Prevalence in affecteds statistically increased over controls. More info...

The prevalence of the variant in affected individuals is significantly increased compared with the prevalence in controls.
Note 1: Relative risk or OR, as obtained from case–control studies, is >5.0, and the confidence interval around the estimate of relative risk or OR does not include 1.0. See the article for detailed guidance. Note 2: In instances of very rare variants where case–control studies may not reach statistical significance, the prior observation of the variant in multiple unrelated patients with the same phenotype, and its absence in controls, may be used as moderate level of evidence

Comment
  • Not applied
  • Apply PS4
  • Apply PS4_moderate
  • Apply PS4_supporting
  • May be able to apply with further evidence
PM2 Absent in population databases. More info...

Absent from controls (or at extremely low frequency if recessive) in Exome Sequencing Project, 1000 Genomes Project, or Exome Aggregation Consortium.
Caveat: Population data for insertions/deletions may be poorly called by next-generation sequencing.

Comment
  • Not applied
  • Apply PM2
  • Apply PM2_strong
  • Apply PM2_supporting
  • May be able to apply with further evidence
BA1/ BS1 MAF is too high for disorder. More info...

Allele frequency is greater than expected for disorder.
BA1- Allele frequency is >5% in Exome Sequencing Project, 1000 Genomes Project, or Exome Aggregation Consortium. BS1- Allele frequency is greater than expected for disorder.

Comment
  • Not applied
  • Apply BA1
  • Apply BS1
  • Apply BS1_moderate
  • Apply BS1_supporting
  • May be able to apply with further evidence
BS2 Observation in controls inconsistent with disease penetrance. More info...

Observed in a healthy adult individual for a recessive (homozygous), dominant (heterozygous), or X-linked (hemizygous) disorder, with full penetrance expected at an early age

Comment
  • Not applied
  • Apply BS2
  • Apply BS2_moderate
  • Apply BS2_supporting
  • May be able to apply with further evidence
